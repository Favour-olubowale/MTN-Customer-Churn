# MTN-Customer-Churn

## Project Overview
This project analyzes customer churn patterns and their impact on revenue within a telecommunications company. The dashboard highlights key metrics such as total customers, churn rate, revenue generated, and revenue lost due to churn. By examining factors like customer tenure, satisfaction levels, and subscription plans, the analysis provides insights that can help improve customer retention and reduce churn.

## Tools Used
- Microsoft Excel
- Pivot Tables
- Data Cleaning
- Dashboard Design

## Dataset
The dataset contains 975 mtncustomer churn records including Customer_ID,Year_of_Purchase,Month_of_purchase,Unit_Price,Number_of_Times etc. 
 

## Observation
-Customer churn is relatively high at 29%.
This indicates that nearly one third of MTN customers discontinue the service within the observed period, pointing to challenges in customer retention and overall service experience.

-Customers with shorter tenure show higher churn tendencies.
Churn is more pronounced among customers in their early months, suggesting that issues during onboarding or initial service experience may influence customers’ decisions to leave.

-Customer satisfaction has a strong influence on churn behavior.
Customers with lower satisfaction ratings are more likely to churn, highlighting satisfaction as a key driver of customer retention.

-Churn varies significantly across subscription plans.
Certain subscription plans record higher churn rates, indicating possible dissatisfaction with pricing, data limits, or perceived value of those plans.

-Revenue loss due to churn is substantial.
Out of ₦199.35M total revenue generated, ₦58M has been lost to churned customers, showing the financial impact of customer attrition on the business.

-Device type has minimal impact on churn.
Most customers use Mobile SIM cards, and there is no significant difference in churn behavior based on device type, suggesting churn is driven more by service factors than hardware.

-Data usage patterns do not significantly differentiate churned customers.
Both churned and non-churned customers show similar data usage distribution, indicating that churn is not primarily driven by data consumption levels.
  

  ## Recommendation
-Strengthen early-stage customer engagement.
MTN should focus on improving the customer experience within the first six months through better onboarding, usage guidance, and proactive customer support.

-Prioritize customer satisfaction improvement initiatives.
Regular monitoring of satisfaction scores and prompt resolution of customer complaints can help reduce churn driven by poor service experiences.

-Review and optimize high-churn subscription plans.
Subscription plans with high churn should be reassessed to improve pricing structures, data offerings, or additional benefits to increase perceived value.

-Implement targeted retention strategies.
Customers with low satisfaction ratings, short tenure, or high-risk subscription plans should be targeted with personalized retention offers and incentives.

-Promote longer-term and loyalty-based plans.
Encouraging customers to move to longer-term plans through discounts and loyalty rewards can help improve retention and customer lifetime value.

-Leverage customer feedback to drive service improvements.
Common reasons for churn should be analyzed and addressed to prevent future customer losses.
